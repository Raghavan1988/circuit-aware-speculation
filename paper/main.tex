\begin{abstract}
In speculative decoding, every control signal in deployed use today is computed
\emph{after} draft tokens exist: draft-model entropy, agreement heads on draft
hidden states, or acceptance history. We show that the \emph{target} model's own
cached representation at the frontier position---the residual-stream state at
the last verified token, a free byproduct of the previous verification pass---%
predicts whether the \emph{next} round's first drafted token will be accepted,
\emph{before any draft compute is spent}. Against a hardened pre-round baseline
(previous-round target entropy, top-1/top-2 margin, recent-acceptance history,
and domain), a linear probe on this representation adds
$+0.05$--$+0.10$ AUROC, replicated across two model families (Qwen2.5-7B/0.5B,
Llama-3.1-8B/3.2-1B) and two corpora (4-domain and 7-axis), on frozen test
splits under two protocols including a strict dev$\to$test transfer in which no
parameter, scaler, or calibrator ever touches test data. Equal-capacity random
controls collapse to chance-level additions, Platt-calibrated probabilities are
decision-usable (test ECE $0.016$--$0.037$), and the probe costs ${\sim}16\,\mu
s$ per round (${\sim}0.015\%$ of a decode round). Steering along the probe
direction disrupts realized acceptance $2$--$10\times$ more than norm-matched
random and shuffled controls, dose-dependently and beyond the induced entropy
change, in both model families---evidence that the representation is causally
load-bearing for acceptance, not epiphenomenal. The signal is
\emph{first-token only}: per-length survival probes show no usable lift for run
length ($k\ge4$), cleanly separating this result from draft-length prediction.
Independently, a behavioral acceptance atlas---frozen-tested against
pre-registered criteria---shows that acceptance is strongly structured by token
category (structural tokens $+0.12$ to $+0.21$ over entity tokens, CI-clean in
all $15$ domain cells), while an absolute-position phase axis does not survive.
We position the finding as an honest diagnostic: it complements, rather than
replaces, within-round draft-entropy control, and its decision value
concentrates in expensive-draft regimes.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Speculative decoding (SD) accelerates autoregressive generation by letting a
cheap drafter propose tokens that the target model verifies in parallel,
guaranteeing exact-match (lossless) outputs under greedy
decoding~[arXiv:2211.17192; arXiv:2302.01318]. Its efficiency is governed by
acceptance: rejected drafts waste compute. A large literature therefore
predicts or adapts to acceptance---but, to our knowledge, every deployed or
published control signal is computed \emph{within} the round, after draft
compute has already been spent: draft-model entropy stops
[arXiv:2411.18462; arXiv:2410.18351], learned heads on \emph{draft} hidden
states [arXiv:2405.19715; arXiv:2607.05147], bandits over configurations from
acceptance history [arXiv:2505.15141], or verification-time judges on drafted
tokens [arXiv:2501.19309].

This paper asks a strictly earlier question: \emph{before} the drafter runs at
all, does the serving stack already hold information about whether drafting
will pay off? A natural candidate is free: when round $r{-}1$'s verification
pass commits tokens, the target model's forward pass has already materialized
its residual-stream representation at the \emph{frontier}---the last committed
position. That state exists in memory before round $r$ drafts; reading it costs
nothing beyond a probe.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{A pre-round diagnostic signal.} A linear probe on the target's
cached frontier representation (four layers) predicts first-token acceptance of
the upcoming round with $+0.05$--$+0.10$ AUROC over a hardened pre-round
baseline (previous-round target entropy, top-1/top-2 margin, acceptance-history
EMA, domain one-hots), Platt-calibrated, with equal-capacity random and
norm-matched controls collapsing to chance-level additions
(\S\ref{sec:results-main}).
\item \textbf{Replication and frozen-test discipline.} The result replicates
across two model families and two corpora (three settings), on frozen test
splits, under two protocols---including a strict dev$\to$test transfer in which
the probe, scaler, imputation statistics, domain vocabulary, and calibrator are
all frozen on development data and the test split is scored exactly once
(\S\ref{sec:protocol}, \S\ref{sec:results-main}).
\item \textbf{A causal check at the representation level.} Forward-hook
steering along the probe direction, at matched norms and graded doses, disrupts
realized acceptance $2$--$10\times$ more than random and shuffled controls,
dose-dependently, beyond the induced entropy change, at all four probed layers,
in both families (\S\ref{sec:causal}). We deliberately describe this as
representation-level causal evidence, not a mechanistic account.
\item \textbf{Behavioral structure in acceptance itself.} Independently of any
probe, an acceptance atlas resolved by token category shows that structurally
forced tokens (delimiters, operators, numbers) are accepted $+0.12$ to $+0.21$
more often than entity and reasoning-transition tokens---CI-clean in all $15$
domain cells across the three settings, on a frozen test split against criteria
pre-registered before unblinding (\S\ref{sec:atlas}). The companion
\emph{phase} axis does not survive and is reported as narrowed out.
\item \textbf{Honest boundaries.} The signal is first-token only: per-length
survival probes are null-to-harmful at $k\ge4$, so the representation does
\emph{not} support run-length selection (\S\ref{sec:length})---differentiating
it from draft-length predictors [arXiv:2412.18910]. A matched study on
\emph{draft}-side hidden states fails to beat cheap surface signals
(\S\ref{sec:negatives}), cheap compressions of the frontier state lose most of
the lift, and the decision value of the pre-round signal concentrates in
expensive-draft regimes; within-round entropy control captures more of the
adaptive-length headroom (\S\ref{sec:systems}).
\end{enumerate}

Combined, these results occupy a cell that a verified literature sweep
(\S\ref{sec:related}) indicates is empty: \emph{pre-round} timing $\times$
\emph{target-side cached} representation $\times$ \emph{lossless} exact-match
SD $\times$ \emph{calibrated} probability $\times$ \emph{baseline-controlled}
against free entropy/margin/history signals $\times$ \emph{independent} drafter
$\times$ \emph{skip-capable} decision $\times$ \emph{first-token} target.

\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Within-round draft-side control.} Draft-entropy stopping rules
[arXiv:2411.18462; arXiv:2410.18351], learned acceptance heads on post-draft
hidden states [arXiv:2405.19715], confidence-scheduled verification from
signals emitted during drafting [arXiv:2607.05147], draft-confidence
continue/stop loops [arXiv:2503.05096], compression-aware run-length selection
from draft entropy/confidence [arXiv:2605.02888], dynamic lookahead
[arXiv:2405.04304], post-hoc divergence diagnostics [arXiv:2509.01083], tree
pruning and construction [arXiv:2502.13652; arXiv:2402.12374;
arXiv:2607.16673], RL coordination of draft depth and verification size
[arXiv:2603.01639], and companion-model verification-length adaptation
[arXiv:2509.24328] all consume signals that exist only after draft compute
begins. History-only bandits over configurations [arXiv:2505.15141] use a
signal we include in our baseline.

\paragraph{Pre-draft signals from the target.} AdaEAGLE
[arXiv:2412.18910] is the closest prior: its length predictor consumes exactly
our signal class---the target's final-layer state at the last validated
position, pre-draft---but regresses \emph{draft length} (uncalibrated scalar
regression), is deliberately logit-free (no entropy or margin baselines),
offers no skip action, and operates a trained EAGLE-style head
[arXiv:2401.15077] rather than an independent drafter. Notably, its own
ablation finds length \emph{classification} inferior---convergent with our
finding that the frontier state supports a first-token decision, not length
selection. Cache-draft payoff routing [arXiv:2606.01019] estimates accepted
length of a retrieved draft from cache/history features (no hidden states, no
skip). Drafter-paradigm routing from target-side signals [arXiv:2606.07710]
chooses \emph{which} drafter to run, not \emph{whether} drafting pays.

\paragraph{Verification-time judgment and relaxed acceptance.} Judge Decoding
[arXiv:2501.19309] scores already-drafted tokens with a head on target
embeddings and relaxes losslessness; semantic-acceptance probing of draft and
target states during verification [arXiv:2602.03708] likewise changes the
correctness contract. We keep exact-match verification and act strictly
pre-round.

\paragraph{Acceptance analysis and theory.} Domain-grain acceptance analyses
[arXiv:2604.14682] document task-level variation we control for via domain
one-hots. An acceptance-certificate theory [arXiv:2606.30265] derives sharp
margin-based bounds for strict greedy acceptance in which the target's
top-1/top-2 margin carries the governing variable---formalizing why our
baseline \emph{must} include margin and entropy for the incremental claim to
be meaningful.

\paragraph{Pre-generation probing methodology.} Linear probes on
pre-generation activations predicting answer or code correctness
[arXiv:2509.10625; arXiv:2606.14530] establish the general probing template we
adapt; reported failures of linear directions to transfer across tasks
[arXiv:2506.08572] motivate our cross-family, cross-corpus replication design.

\section{Setting}
\label{sec:setting}

\paragraph{Lossless speculative decoding.} A target model $T$ and independent
drafter $D$ (same tokenizer) generate greedily. In round $r$, $D$ proposes up
to $L$ tokens extending the committed prefix; $T$ verifies them in one forward
pass; the longest prefix agreeing with $T$'s greedy argmax is committed, plus
one bonus token. Output tokens are exactly $T$'s greedy continuation.

\paragraph{Frontier representation.} Let $x_{1:t}$ be the committed prefix
after round $r{-}1$. Verification of round $r{-}1$ already computed $T$'s
residual stream over the prefix; we denote by
$h_\ell(t)\in\mathbb{R}^{d}$ the layer-$\ell$ residual-stream state at the
frontier (last committed) position. Our candidate signal for round $r$ is the
concatenation $\phi(t)=[h_{\ell}(t)]_{\ell\in\{6,12,18,24\}}$
($d_{\mathrm{model}}=3584$ for the Qwen pair, $4096$ for Llama; $\phi$ has
$14{,}336$ resp.\ $16{,}384$ dimensions). $\phi(t)$ exists in memory before
round $r$ drafts.

\paragraph{Labels.} The prediction target is \emph{first-token acceptance}:
whether round $r$'s first drafted token matches $T$'s greedy argmax
(equivalently, accepted length $\ge 1$). Per-length survival labels
$\mathbf{1}[A_r\ge k]$ for $k\in\{1,2,4,6,8\}$ use the round's counterfactual
full-information acceptance record (the engine logs agreement for all proposed
positions, independent of the executed policy).

\paragraph{Pre-round baseline (the bar).} All lifts are \emph{incremental}
over a frozen baseline containing every free pre-round signal we could
identify: (i) recent-acceptance history (EMA over strictly prior rounds); (ii)
previous-round target next-token entropy at the frontier; (iii) previous-round
target top-1/top-2 margin; (iv) per-column missing flags with mean imputation;
(v) domain one-hots. Items (ii)--(iii) are free byproducts of verification and,
per certificate theory [arXiv:2606.30265], carry the governing variable for
greedy acceptance; item (v) removes the confound that the representation might
merely re-identify domain.

\section{Experimental protocol}
\label{sec:protocol}

\paragraph{Models and corpora.} Primary pair: Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct (target) /
Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct (drafter). Replication pair: Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct /
Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct. Model revisions are pinned. Corpus v1: $644$ prompts
over four domains (chat, code, math, summarization). Corpus v2: $1{,}494$
prompts over seven axes (adding translation, retrieval-grounded QA, structured
generation). Decode traces are sealed fixed-length ($L{=}8$) sweeps,
$\mathrm{max\_new}=256$, bf16, single-request batches on A100-class
accelerators. The three settings reported are (Qwen, v1), (Qwen, v2),
(Llama, v1).

\paragraph{Engine validity.} The engine passes bit-identity gates against
target-only greedy decoding in fp32 (all tests, both families; e.g.\ $118/118$
on the Llama pair), including skip, stop-rule, and end-of-sequence edge cases.
In bf16, parallel-verify vs.\ sequential-decode arithmetic differs at the
${\sim}0.7\%$/token level through argmax ties; per-request equivalence status
is logged, and acceptance labels remain exact in-context ground truth
regardless of trajectory divergence.

\paragraph{Capture.} For each setting we capture $\phi(t)$ at every
proposal-bearing round's frontier position by teacher-forcing the target over
the committed prefix, for $400$ prompts sampled domain-stratified round-robin
(a sorted-truncation sampler in an early capture produced ${\sim}2$
domains/run and was replaced; all reported captures are stratified). Row
counts: $23{,}568$ (Qwen-v1), $14{,}122$ (Qwen-v2), $19{,}269$ (Llama)
$\times$ four layers, stored fp16.

\paragraph{Splits and fitting.} All splits are prompt-grouped (50/50
dev/test, fixed seed); no token- or row-level random splits anywhere.
Probes are $\ell_2$-regularized logistic models ($C{=}0.1$; chosen on dev for
convergence and run-to-run reproducibility---under-regularized fits were
observed to be BLAS-thread-dependent), fit under GroupKFold ($5$ folds) with
within-fold standardization. Every candidate is scored with two
\emph{equal-capacity controls} (i.i.d.\ Gaussian columns of the candidate's
exact dimensionality, raw and per-column std-matched): a ``lift'' that random
columns of equal capacity can reproduce is not a lift. Uncertainty: 95\%
prompt-grouped bootstrap CIs on \deltaAUROC{} ($1{,}000$ resamples; $2{,}000$
for length probes) with \pdle{} reported. Calibration: a single global Platt
map on out-of-fold scores (monotone; AUROC-preserving), so decision metrics
(ECE, regret) are computed on calibrated probabilities.

\paragraph{Two frozen test protocols.} Development-set selection identified
exactly one surviving candidate (the raw frontier representation; see
\S\ref{sec:negatives} for rejected variants). The frozen test split was then
evaluated once per protocol, single pre-registered spec, no test-side
selection:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{P1, within-test OOF:} the dev-frozen pipeline (features, $C$,
domain control, seed) is run under prompt-grouped OOF \emph{within} the test
rows. Nothing is selected on test, but fold models are fit on test rows.
\item \textbf{P2, strict dev$\to$test transfer:} scaler, probe, control
columns, imputation means, domain vocabulary, and Platt calibrator are all fit
on dev only; test rows are scored exactly once. No parameter of any kind is
fit on test.
\end{itemize}
P2 is the deployment-faithful protocol; P1 is reported for comparability with
the development analysis. Reported numbers reproduce across independent
containers to ${\sim}{\pm}0.001$ AUROC (BLAS reduction-order noise; an order
of magnitude below CI half-widths).

\subsection{The signal is first-token only}
\label{sec:length}

Per-length survival probes $P(A\ge k)$ (Table~\ref{tab:length}) localize the
information: the lift is strong and CI-clean at $k{=}1$ in all three settings
and is null-to-\emph{harmful} at $k\ge4$ (significantly worse than baseline on
v2/Llama at $k{=}8$, where domain and history already predict long runs). A
survival-driven length controller correspondingly fails to beat a
baseline-driven one except marginally at a single draft-cost point. The
frontier state answers ``will speculation connect at all next round?''---not
``for how long?''. This cleanly separates the contribution from pre-draft
\emph{length} regression [arXiv:2412.18910], whose own ablations show length
classification underperforming, and motivates the skip/admission decision as
the natural consumer of the signal.

\subsection{Steering the direction disrupts acceptance}
\label{sec:causal}


\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig_dose.pdf}
\caption{Dose--response steering of the target's frontier residual stream, at
all four probed layers, in both families for which interventions were run
(Qwen-v2 was not intervened on and has no panel). Steering along the fitted
acceptance direction collapses realized acceptance dose-dependently, in an
inverted-U peaking at $\alpha{=}0$, while norm-matched random and shuffled
directions at identical dose stay nearly flat.}
\label{fig:dose}
\end{figure}

Predictive lift alone cannot distinguish a load-bearing representation from a
correlated shadow. On held-out test rounds we therefore steer the target's
residual stream at the frontier position with forward hooks:
$h_\ell \leftarrow h_\ell + \alpha\,\sigma_\ell\,\hat d_\ell$, where
$\hat d_\ell$ is the first-token acceptance direction fit on dev,
$\sigma_\ell$ the mean frontier-state norm (natural dose scale), and
$\alpha\in\{-2,-1,0,+1,+2\}$. Controls at every layer: norm-matched random
directions and shuffled directions; a no-op fidelity check validates the hook
site (fidelity $0.95$ Qwen-v1 / $1.00$ Llama). Steering along $\hat d_\ell$
disrupts realized first-token acceptance $2$--$10\times$ more than the
matched controls, dose-dependently (inverted-U peaking at $\alpha{=}0$), at
all four probed layers, in both families; on Llama, real disruption is
$0.32$--$0.40$ while controls are ${\approx}0$. Within narrow bins of the
induced entropy change the real-vs-control gap persists, so the effect is not
routed through entropy alone. We interpret this strictly at the representation
level: the probed direction causally controls the target's next-token
agreement-ability. We make no claim about internal computational structure.

\subsection{Cost and decision relevance}
\label{sec:systems}

\begin{figure}[t]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.66\linewidth]{figures/fig_regret.pdf}
\caption{Decision relevance of the pre-round signal as draft cost varies
(frozen test, strict protocol; higher is better). Filled markers mark costs
where the recorded CI clears zero. The signal is inert while drafting is cheap
---every calibrated model simply elects ``always draft''---and pays only once
draft cost approaches or exceeds verify cost. Qwen-v1 clears the CI bar at a
single cost point, the protocol sensitivity we report in the text.}
\label{fig:regret}
\end{figure}


\paragraph{Cost.} Reading $\phi(t)$ is free on the capture path (the states
are byproducts of verification); the probe is one $14$k-dimensional dot
product: ${\sim}16\,\mu s$ per round on our measurement setup,
${\sim}0.015\%$ of a decode round. The honest caveat is engineering, not
compute: serving engines do not currently retain multi-layer frontier states,
so productionizing requires exposing them (cheap) or a compact sufficient
statistic (open; \S\ref{sec:negatives}).

\paragraph{Decision proxy.} Sweeping a calibrated skip/draft decision across
draft-cost ratios: at cheap draft cost ($\le0.2\times$ verify) every
calibrated model elects ``always draft'' and no pre-round signal changes any
decision---consistent with our finding that adding a skip arm under cheap
serving costs \emph{hurts} ($-0.3$ to $-0.7\%$). At draft cost
$\ge 1\times$ verify, the probe's regret reduction is CI-robust across a cost
range on Qwen-v2 and Llama under both protocols (and on Qwen-v1 under P1;
under P2 it is CI-robust only at one cost---a protocol sensitivity we report).
The pre-round signal is decision-relevant where drafting is expensive
(launch-bound drafters, large drafters, congested batch serving), and
diagnostic elsewhere.

\paragraph{Where pre-round control fits.} For adaptive \emph{length}, a
within-round draft-entropy stop remains stronger: it beats the best fixed
length by $+6.7$--$+11.2\%$ serving-efficiency across our three settings
(e.g.\ $+11.2\%$, 95\% CI $[+10.3\%, +12.1\%]$ on Qwen-v1), while a pre-round
frontier-entropy gate recovers only ${\sim}1/3$--$1/2$ of that gain. The two
signals are complementary by timing: pre-round admission/skip and scheduling
vs.\ within-round stopping.

\subsection{Supporting negative results}
\label{sec:negatives}

Three negatives sharpen the claim. (i) \emph{Draft-side hidden states do not
beat surface signals}: in a matched incremental study on the same traces, a
linear probe on the \emph{drafter's} residual stream (best layer) reaches
$0.803$ AUROC vs.\ a $0.870$ surface stack (entropy, margin, history,
position), adding at most $+0.006$ when combined---so the pre-round result is
not ``probes beat baselines'' generically; the target-side, pre-round framing
is doing the work. (ii) \emph{Cheap compressions lose the signal}: low-rank
projections ($k{=}16$), norm summaries, layer-alignment features, and drift
velocities add only $+0.01$--$+0.04$ and are inconsistent across settings;
only the full representation clears the bar in all three. A compact sufficient
statistic is the main open engineering question. (iii) \emph{History-only
control is weak}: the acceptance-history EMA is already in our baseline, and
stationary bandit controllers over length arms [arXiv:2505.15141] learn the
best single arm and therefore tie the best fixed length by construction in our
replays (serving-efficiency $2.4015$ vs.\ $2.4022$ for best-fixed, while the
contextual entropy stop reaches $2.6715$)---context, not competition, for
representation-level signals.

\section{What acceptance is about: the token-category atlas}
\label{sec:atlas}

The preceding sections establish that a pre-round signal exists and is
causally load-bearing. This section asks a complementary, purely
\emph{behavioral} question, with no probes and no internal states: does
acceptance itself have legible structure? We answer with an
\emph{acceptance atlas}---acceptance rate resolved by token category and by
generation phase, computed on the same sealed traces, with prompt-grouped
bootstrap CIs. Labels are the counterfactual per-position agreement record
(overlapping category taxonomy v1.0.0; absolute-position phase bins).

\paragraph{Domain control.} Domain marginals reproduce the domain-grain
acceptance axis reported previously [arXiv:2604.14682] (e.g.\ on Qwen-v1 dev:
summarization $0.657 <$ chat $0.700 <$ code $0.813 <$ math $0.875$). We treat
that axis as a \emph{control} that our pipeline recovers; our contribution is
the finer category-within-domain structure that survives conditioning on it.

\subsection{Structural tokens are accepted far more often than entity tokens}
\label{sec:atlas-contrast}

We pre-specified a single contrast on development data---HIGH
(\texttt{code\_delimiter}, \texttt{operator}, \texttt{number}, pooled) minus
LOW (\texttt{named\_entity}, \texttt{reasoning\_transition}, pooled)---together
with criteria T1--T4 and their outcome mapping, and committed them before the
test split was unblinded. Table~\ref{tab:atlas} reports the one-shot test

The contrast is large ($+0.12$ to $+0.21$ acceptance), and it is not domain

families---\texttt{code\_delimiter}, \texttt{operator}, \texttt{number},

commitment. \texttt{reasoning\_transition} is low on the Qwen pair only.

Llama-code was flat on dev ($-0.006$, at a $0.913$ acceptance ceiling) and was

$+0.0544$ with $\pdle=0.027$ (percentile CI lower bound $-0.002$): it clears
the pre-registered $p<0.05$ bar, and we flag it as the margin to watch in any
re-run.

\subsection{The phase axis does not survive}
\label{sec:atlas-phase}

The claim as originally posed had a second half---variation across generation
\emph{phases}---and it did not survive. On both Qwen settings the
absolute-position phase spread is decisively flat on test ($0.0084$ and
$0.0036$, against a pre-registered $\le 0.03$ criterion). The Llama pair shows
a monotone rise (prefix $0.794 \to$ mid $0.824 \to$ late $0.853$), but this is
one family, a length/mix confound is plausible, and we record it descriptively
without a claim. We therefore narrow the atlas result to the category axis
only: absolute-position phase bins carry no cross-family acceptance structure
at this grain.

A descriptive anomaly, recorded rather than explained: on the Llama pair the
\texttt{special} category accepts at $0.007$ ($n{=}883$), most likely a
chat-template or special-token handling divergence between the two pairs
rather than an acceptance phenomenon. It is excluded from the contrast and
flagged for separate investigation.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

First-token scope (no run-length claim; the length-aware controller does not
benefit). Full-representation scope (the deployable-cheap variant is open).
Greedy exact-match SD only; sampling-based acceptance untested. Two model
families, one drafter per family; trained-head speculators (EAGLE-class) are
out of scope by design (independent-drafter cell). Layers $\{6,12,18,24\}$
were selected for the drafter's depth and inherited by the deeper targets; a
target-tuned layer sweep may strengthen (or localize) the result. Decision
analysis is a calibrated proxy over sealed traces; end-to-end wall-clock
evaluation with the probe in the serving path---our systems gate---has not
been run and no wall-clock claim is made. Batch-size-1 harness; batch
interference unmeasured. Calibration under the strict protocol degrades on one
setting (reported). bf16 argmax-tie trajectory divergence is characterized and
logged rather than eliminated. The atlas (\S\ref{sec:atlas}) is scoped to an
overlapping category taxonomy (v1.0.0) over counterfactual fixed-length
agreement labels, two families and two corpora; its phase half is narrowed out
rather than supported, and it is a behavioral description that we do not link
to the probe's internal evidence.

\section{Conclusion}

Before any draft compute is spent, the target model's cached frontier
representation already carries calibrated, causally load-bearing,
first-token-specific information about whether the next speculation round will
connect---beyond entropy, margin, history, and domain, in two model families
and two corpora, under frozen-test discipline. We offer it as an honest
diagnostic with a precisely mapped boundary: a pre-round admission signal that
complements within-round control, decision-relevant exactly where drafting is
expensive. Separately, and without appeal to internal states, acceptance itself
proves to be strongly structured by token category and---at the grain we
tested---not by generation phase.

\section*{Reproducibility statement}

Every reported number is generated by scripts from sealed, immutable decode
traces and activation captures; no statistic is hand-derived. Splits are
prompt-grouped with fixed seeds; the frozen-test protocol was pre-registered
(single spec, criteria fixed before unblinding) and executed once per
protocol; first-pass artifacts are archived alongside re-runs. Cross-container
reproducibility of headline numbers is ${\pm}0.001$ AUROC. Code, sealed
artifacts, and the full claims ledger (including all negative results and two
integrity disclosures) are prepared for release under a staged policy.
